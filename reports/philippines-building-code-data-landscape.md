# Philippines Building Code Data Landscape

Date: 2026-07-04

## Purpose

This is the Step 0 landscape scan for the **Philippines Building Code Evidence
Auditor**. The goal is to understand what public evidence exists before designing
the final evidence packet schema.

The product should be an evidence auditor, not a compliance certifier. It may
show public evidence, gaps, contradictions, and manual follow-up paths. It must
not claim that a building is legally compliant, structurally safe,
earthquake-safe, or fit for occupancy.

## Summary

The workflow is feasible if we frame it correctly.

Public sources can support:

- building-code and structural-code context;
- city OBO/DBO permit process discovery;
- occupancy and building permit requirement checklists;
- contractor and professional verification routes;
- earthquake damage, closure, red-tag/yellow-tag, inspection, and incident
  history;
- FOI and manual request paths.

Public sources usually cannot prove:

- that one specific building fully complies with all codes;
- that its structural design is adequate;
- that it is currently earthquake-safe;
- that it has a valid occupancy permit if no public record is found;
- that missing public evidence means noncompliance.

## Six Research Lanes

### 1. Standards Lane

**Can do:** collect the rules and standards context.

DPWH publishes National Building Code references, PD 1096 materials, fee
references, and NBCDO memoranda. ASEP publicly describes its role and
publications, including National Structural Code volumes and referral-code
context.

**Can’t do:** determine whether a specific building design complies without
plans, calculations, and professional review.

Key sources:

- [DPWH National Building Code references](https://www.dpwh.gov.ph/dpwh/nbc)
- [DPWH PD 1096 page](https://www.dpwh.gov.ph/dpwh/references/laws_codes_orders/PD1096)
- [DPWH PD 1096 PDF](https://www.dpwh.gov.ph/DPWH/files/nbc/PD.pdf)
- [ASEP about page](https://aseponline.org/about/)
- [DPWH NBCDO MC 01 s. 2025](https://www.dpwh.gov.ph/DPWH/files/NBCDO/NBCDO_MC_01_s2025.pdf)

### 2. LGU / OBO Lane

**Can do:** find city-specific building-office authority, services, forms,
citizen charters, application portals, and request paths.

Quezon City says its DBO implements the National Building Code, the local
building code, and their IRRs, and processes building permits, ancillary permits,
certificates of occupancy, and CFEIs. Davao City lists building permit,
occupancy permit, annual inspection, certification, and complaints on National
Building Code violations as OCBO functions.

**Can’t do:** rely on a single national searchable public database for
building-specific permits. Public availability varies by LGU.

Key sources:

- [Quezon City Department of the Building Official](https://quezoncity.gov.ph/departments/department-of-the-building-official/)
- [Davao City Office of the City Building Official](https://davaocity.gov.ph/departments/infrastructure/office-of-the-building-official/)
- [Cebu City building application forms](https://www.cebucity.gov.ph/building-application-forms/)
- [Pasig building permit checklist PDF](https://assets.pasigcity.gov.ph/storage/downloadables/2024/07/29/66a73e828c9fa1722236546CHECKLIST%20OF%20REQUIREMENTS%20-%20BUILDING%20PERMIT%20APPLICATION.pdf)
- [Pasig occupancy permit checklist PDF](https://assets.pasigcity.gov.ph/storage/downloadables/2024/07/29/66a73de9b38e11722236393CHECKLIST%20OF%20REQUIREMENTS%20-%20OCCUPANCY%20PERMIT%20APPLICATION.pdf)
- [Taguig local building office citizen charter PDF](https://www.taguig.gov.ph/assets/pdfform/citizens_charter/local_building_office/ARTA-Local_Building_Office.pdf)

### 3. Building Identity Lane

**Can do:** normalize the target building or establishment before compliance
evidence search.

The agent should separate:

- building name;
- tenant or establishment name;
- exact address;
- owner;
- operator;
- developer;
- jurisdiction;
- use type.

SEC services and LGU business-permit pages can help identify operators and
business entities, but some official company records involve search, payment, or
manual workflows.

**Can’t do:** assume a mall, hotel, tenant, tower, owner, and developer are the
same entity.

Key sources:

- [Quezon City Business Permits and Licensing Department](https://quezoncity.gov.ph/departments/business-permit-and-licensing-department-2/)
- [Taguig Business Permits and Licensing Office](https://www.taguig.gov.ph/business/)
- [SEC eSPARC](https://esparc.sec.gov.ph/)
- [SEC Express](https://secexpress.ph/)

### 4. Contractor / Professional Lane

**Can do:** verify named contractors or professionals when the workflow finds
them.

PCAB has public contractor license verification routes, including regular,
special, pakyaw, exemptions, and suspended/revoked records. PRC has public
license verification for registered professionals, though searches often need
exact names, license numbers, or other details.

**Can’t do:** invent contractor, architect, or engineer names when they are not
publicly tied to the building.

Key sources:

- [PCAB license verification portal](https://pcabgovph.com/verify/)
- [CIAP PCAB verification listing](https://construction.gov.ph/directory-listings/directory-listings-pcab/pcab-online-license-verification-website/)
- [PCAB suspended/revoked licenses](https://pcabgovph.com/verify/suspendedlicenses.php)
- [PRC license verification](https://verification.prc.gov.ph/)
- [PRC FOI license verification guidance](https://www.foi.gov.ph/agencies/prc/license-verification-prc-739166038591/)

### 5. Incident / Safety History Lane

**Can do:** find public incident history and official or reputable reports about
earthquake damage, closure, red/yellow tagging, inspection, repair, retrofit, or
collapse.

Davao City has public pages about red-tagged and yellow-tagged structures after
rapid visual inspections. PIA/PNA and news sources report post-earthquake
inspections and closures. Legal references also identify dangerous-building
abatement powers under PD 1096.

**Can’t do:** use news or social media alone as proof of current safety.

Key sources:

- [Davao City red/yellow tag announcement](https://davaocity.gov.ph/local-government/davao-city-issues-red-yellow-tags-on-earthquake-affected-buildings/)
- [MindaNews Davao damaged buildings report](https://mindanews.com/top-stories/2025/10/davao-city-building-official-bars-use-of-buildings-severely-damaged-by-quake/)
- [PIA Butuan safety inspection report](https://pia.gov.ph/news/butuan-city-conducts-safety-inspection-following-intensity-5-earthquake/)
- [PNA Cebu earthquake inspection report](https://www.pna.gov.ph/articles/1259940)
- [DPWH post-earthquake inspection advice](https://dpwh.gov.ph/DPWH/news/11209)
- [Supreme Court E-Library PD 1096 dangerous-building provisions](https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/11/53320)

### 6. FOI / Manual Request Lane

**Can do:** identify records that may exist but need a formal or manual path.

FOI pages show that people request building permits, FSICs, building year,
construction type, location clearances, and related documents. The outcomes vary:
some are successful, some denied, and some require clarification.

**Can’t do:** pretend FOI/manual-only records have been checked by a normal web
search.

Key sources:

- [FOI certified true copy of building permit request](https://www.foi.gov.ph/requests/certified-true-copy-of-building-permit/)
- [FOI BFP denied request listing with FSIC examples](https://www.foi.gov.ph/agencies/bfp/DENIED/)
- [FOI BFP agency page](https://www.foi.gov.ph/agencies/bfp/)
- [FOI SBMA building year and construction type request](https://www.foi.gov.ph/requests/building-year-built-and-construction-type-subic-bay-metropolitan-authority/)
- [FOI USeP location clearance request](https://www.foi.gov.ph/agencies/usep/)
- [FOI AIC Burgundy Empire Tower FSIC request](https://www.foi.gov.ph/requests/copy-of-fsic-2017-2022-of-aic-burgundy-empire-tower/)

## What The Agent Can Automate

- Search and classify official standards sources.
- Find city OBO/DBO pages and permit/occupancy checklists.
- Normalize building identity and jurisdiction.
- Find public contractor/professional/company verification portals.
- Search incident, inspection, closure, red-tag/yellow-tag, and retrofit
  history.
- Produce source cards with freshness, access method, caveats, and confidence.
- Produce manual follow-up questions for records not available online.

## What The Agent Must Not Automate As A Final Claim

- Legal compliance certification.
- Structural safety certification.
- Earthquake safety certification.
- Fitness-for-occupancy determination.
- Inference that missing web evidence means noncompliance.
- Professional evaluation of plans, materials, structural systems, or
  calculations.

## Recommended Next Step

Build the evidence schema around these realities.

The schema should have explicit buckets for:

- `public_evidence_found`;
- `public_evidence_not_found`;
- `manual_request_needed`;
- `conflicting_evidence`;
- `not_assessable_from_public_web`.

That keeps the workflow honest and makes it robust enough for real use.
