# Gate 3 SM Mall of Asia Document-Target Search

Date: 2026-07-05

This is a Gate 3 learning run for the Philippines Building Code Evidence
Auditor. Six read-only agents searched one famous target, **SM Mall of Asia /
SM MOA, Pasay City**, split by document family instead of by establishment.

The goal was to learn the exact forms, PDFs, certificates, and report names the
auditor should hunt for. This is not a compliance audit, structural assessment,
fire-safety finding, occupancy finding, or safety certification.

## Executive Summary

The next Gate 3 search should start from exact document targets, not from vague
phrases like "NSCP compliance."

For SM Mall of Asia, public web search found:

- public process evidence for Pasay building-permit, XERSYS, structural-plan,
  occupancy, FSEC/FSIC, and annual-inspection document routes;
- target identity and sublocation evidence from Pasay City and SM Supermalls;
- partial public evidence from SM Prime company filings and operator statements;
- partial incident/advisory evidence;
- no public target-tied building permit, certificate of occupancy, FSEC, FSIC,
  signed/sealed structural plans, structural computations, boring/soil test,
  annual inspection certificate, repair permit, retrofit permit, or reoccupancy
  clearance.

The default label for target-specific NSCP or structural-code questions remains:

`not_assessable_from_public_web`

unless a target-tied official or professional document is found.

## Strongest Source Context

- [Pasay Building Permit Application PDF](https://pasay.gov.ph/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/pasay-city-external-service-building-permit-application.pdf)
  names the building-permit workflow and document requirements, including the
  Unified Application Form, Civil/Structural plans, structural design analysis
  and computations, boring/soil test, FSEC processing, Compliance Evaluation
  Sheet, claim stub, Order of Payment, Official Receipt, and permit release.
- [Pasay XERSYS](https://eodbbuilding.pasay.gov.ph/Account/Login) shows an
  applicant-facing building-permit workflow with online application,
  application number, claim stub, payment, and release steps. It is not a public
  target-record registry.
- [JMC 2018-01](https://elibrary.judiciary.gov.ph/thebookshelf/showdocs/10/89926)
  defines national building permit, certificate of occupancy, FSEC, and FSIC
  process context.
- [SM Supermalls SM Mall of Asia page](https://www.smsupermalls.com/mall-directory/sm-mall-of-asia/information/)
  confirms the target identity and exposes the sublocation problem: Main Mall,
  South Arcade, North/South Parking Buildings, SMX Convention Center, MOA
  Square, and other labels are all part of the public target surface.
- [SM Prime 2025 offer supplement](https://www.smprime.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/0-SMPH-Series-ABACAD-Bonds-Preliminary-Offer-Supplement-as-of-15-September-2025.pdf)
  gives the strongest indirect corporate lead: a material-permits table listing
  SM Mall of Asia business permit entries in Pasay City. This is not a building
  permit, occupancy certificate, FSIC/FSEC, or structural record.
- [SM Prime 2025 Integrated Report](https://www.smprime.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/SM-PRIME-Integrated-Report-2025-compressed.pdf)
  gives operator engineering/resilience context for the MOA Complex, but it is
  not a signed/sealed engineering packet or OBO record.
- [ESCA Engineers Mall of Asia project page](https://www.escaengineers.com/projects/mall-of-asia)
  is a professional/contractor lead for structural design involvement. It does
  not publish the approved structural plans or calculations.
- [SM Prime 2020 advisory](https://www.smprime.com/latest_news/sm-mall-of-asia-structure-intact-no-structural-damage-says-pasay-city-engineering-office/)
  is partial target-specific advisory evidence. It is not a current structural
  certificate or inspection packet.
- [SM Supermalls BFP Pasay satellite-office article](https://www.smsupermalls.com/whats-new/news/sm-mall-of-asia-and-bfp-pasay-city-launch-first-ever-metro-manila-bfp-satellite-office-at-government-service-express)
  confirms BFP Pasay service presence at SM MOA. It does not publish SM MOA's
  FSIC or FSEC.

## Six-Agent Findings

### Agent 1 - Building Permit Packet

Best status: `manual_request_needed` with `public_process_only` support.

The exact process terms are real: Unified Application Form, claim stub, Order of
Payment, Official Receipt, and permit release. Public sources did not reveal a
target-tied SM MOA building permit packet.

### Agent 2 - Structural Document Packet

Best status: `manual_request_needed`.

The search found process requirements and target-specific engineering leads,
including SM Prime resilience statements and ESCA's structural-design project
page. It did not find signed/sealed civil/structural plans, structural
computations, boring/soil report, professional-of-record packet, or authoritative
NSCP compliance document.

### Agent 3 - Occupancy Packet

Best status: `manual_request_needed`.

National and local process sources define the occupancy packet: Certificate of
Occupancy, Certificate of Completion, approved plans/specifications, as-built
plans when changed, FSEC/FSIC-related materials, professional license copies,
photos, and inspection processes. No target-tied SM MOA occupancy packet was
publicly found.

### Agent 4 - Fire-Safety Packet

Best status: `partial_public_evidence` for BFP presence and fire-safety activity;
`manual_request_needed` for target certificates.

BFP Pasay has a satellite office at SM MOA and public materials refer to
inspection, permit, and renewal services. No target-tied FSEC, FSIC, fire-safety
correction sheet, notice, or inspection report was found.

### Agent 5 - Inspection, Incident, Repair, and Reoccupancy Packet

Best status: `partial_public_evidence`.

Public incident/advisory evidence exists, including old fire/ceiling incident
reports and a 2020 Pasay Engineering Office advisory reposted by SM Prime. The
underlying inspection reports, annual inspection certificates, repair permits,
retrofit permits, closure/tag records, and reoccupancy clearances were not found
publicly.

### Agent 6 - Corporate and Operator Filings Packet

Best status: `partial_public_evidence`.

SM Prime filings and reports provide the best indirect evidence. The 2025 offer
supplement lists SM Mall of Asia business permit entries, and SM Prime reports
describe MOA Complex redevelopment/resilience. These are useful leads, not
primary OBO/BFP/structural/occupancy records.

## Gate 3 Design Changes

Gate 3 should now require these fields before any public-evidence packet:

- `target_subscope`
- `document_target`
- `document_family`
- `likely_custodian`
- `source_reality_status`
- `strongest_public_source`
- `target_specific_result`
- `manual_follow_up`
- `overclaim_boundary`
- `query_log`

The important product change is this: **the auditor searches for exact document
targets first**, then classifies whether each target is public evidence, process
context, a weak lead, or a manual request.

## Overclaim Boundary

This run does not support any claim that SM Mall of Asia is NSCP-compliant,
structurally safe, fire-safe, compliant, noncompliant, fit for occupancy,
permitted, or unpermitted.

The correct conclusion is narrower:

Public web can identify the exact documents to ask for and can surface partial
corporate/operator and incident leads. Primary target-tied building,
occupancy, fire-safety, structural, and current-inspection records remain manual
requests unless an official/professional document is found.
