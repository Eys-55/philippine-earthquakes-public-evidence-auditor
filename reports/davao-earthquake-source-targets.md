# Davao Earthquake Source Targets

Date: 2026-07-05

This is the Davao-first source menu for the Philippines Building Code Evidence
Auditor's earthquake-only questions. It does not certify a building as safe,
unsafe, earthquake-proof, NSCP-compliant, noncompliant, permitted, or
unpermitted. It tells the agent exactly what to search for and when the question
is answerable.

## Bottom Line

The four-question matrix is usable for Davao City, but the Davao search menu
should be more specific than the generic Philippine matrix.

For Davao, the strongest public source is the **Davao City Citizen's Charter
2026 Volume II**, because it names OCBO services and document targets such as:

- `Civil/Structural Permit Form with Civil/Structural Plans`
- `Seismic Analysis`
- `Boring and Plate Load Test Result/Report`
- `Evaluation Sheet`
- `Building Permit Number`
- `Approved plans and documents`
- `Certificate of Occupancy`
- `Certificate of Completion`
- `Certificate of Annual Inspection of Buildings`
- `Certified True Copy of Documents`

For post-earthquake status, Davao official pages strongly support **red tag**,
**yellow tag**, **rapid visual inspection**, **unsafe/not safe for occupancy**,
**restricted use**, and **cleared by OCBO** language. Davao official pages did
not strongly surface public **green tag** wording during this run, so do not
require the word green for Davao unless an official source uses it for the exact
building, date, and scope.

## Custodian

Primary custodian: **Davao City Office of the City Building Official (OCBO)**.

Manual request path: use the Citizen's Charter service **Issuance of Certified
True Copy of Documents** and ask for the exact target-specific record.

Request candidates:

- issued Building Permit
- Civil/Structural Permit
- approved Civil/Structural Plans
- Seismic Analysis
- Evaluation Sheet or structural review sheet
- approved plans and documents with Building Permit Number
- Certificate of Occupancy
- Certificate of Completion
- Certificate of Annual Inspection of Buildings
- inspection report, tag notice, clearance, or lifting notice

## Q1: NSCP / Seismic Design Evidence

Question: Is there target-specific evidence that this Davao building was
designed or reviewed for NSCP earthquake/seismic requirements?

Answerable targets:

| Target | Use |
|---|---|
| Target-specific signed/sealed **Seismic Analysis** | Direct Davao-specific search target |
| Target-specific signed/sealed **Structural Design Analysis** or structural design computations | Direct design evidence |
| Target-specific **Civil/Structural Permit Form with Civil/Structural Plans** | Answerable only if issued, approved, released, or tied to OCBO review |
| Target-specific approved plans/documents with **Building Permit Number** | Strong OCBO review evidence |
| Target-specific OCBO **Evaluation Sheet** showing structural review | Strong review evidence |
| Target-specific issued **Building Permit** referencing approved structural/civil documents or structural review | Strong review evidence |

Not enough alone:

- Davao checklist only
- blank permit forms
- Boring and Plate Load Test Result/Report without structural/seismic design
- PRC ID or PTR without the signed/sealed target document
- OBPS portal page without visible issued/approved target record

## Q2: OBO Structural Permit / Review Evidence

Question: Is there target-specific evidence that this Davao building went
through official OCBO structural permit or plan-review processing?

Answerable targets:

| Target | Use |
|---|---|
| Target-specific issued **Building Permit** | Official permit-processing evidence |
| Approved/released **Civil/Structural Permit Form with Civil/Structural Plans** | Direct Davao OCBO structural document target |
| Approved plans/documents stamped with **Building Permit Number** | Strong approval/release evidence |
| **Evaluation Sheet** showing structural review | Strong review evidence |
| **Comprehensive Correction Sheet** or **Notice of Disapproval** | Proves review happened, not approval |
| **Certificate of Occupancy** tied to approved permits/plans | Useful only with exact building/subscope and permit/plan link |

Not enough alone:

- blank application form
- process checklist
- Order of Payment without issued permit, approval, or review disposition
- Certificate of Annual Inspection without original permit/review records

## Q3: Latest Post-Earthquake Tag Status

Question: After a specific earthquake, what is the official Davao tag or
inspection status for this building or affected portion?

Answerable targets:

| Target | Use |
|---|---|
| City Government of Davao article/post/list naming the building and tag/status | Strong public answer |
| OCBO red tag notice or red-tagged list | Strong public answer |
| OCBO yellow tag notice or yellow-tagged list | Strong public answer |
| City Engineer inspection result saying unsafe/not safe for occupancy, restricted, cordoned, or usable | Strong public answer |
| OCBO/PICE rapid visual inspection result | Strong only when OCBO/LGU publishes or adopts it |
| Official clearance/lifting update stating new status | Strong if exact scope/date is clear |

Davao terms to search:

- `red tag notice`
- `red tagged`
- `yellow tag notice`
- `yellow tagged`
- `rapid visual inspection`
- `unsafe for occupancy`
- `not safe for occupancy`
- `restricted use`
- `entry or use prohibited`
- `cleared by OCBO`
- `before re-occupancy`

Green tag rule: do not require `green tag` for Davao. Accept green, inspected,
safe for occupancy, cleared, or equivalent only if an official source says it
for the exact target, date, and scope.

## Q4: Latest Clearance After Damage Or Tag

Question: If the Davao building was tagged, damaged, closed, or restricted, is
there target-specific official clearance allowing use or re-occupancy?

Answerable targets:

| Target | Use |
|---|---|
| Official OCBO/LGU notice saying the building was **cleared by OCBO** | Strong clearance answer |
| Official notice saying re-occupancy, occupancy, entry, or use may resume | Strong clearance answer |
| Official green-tag update or tag lifting notice | Strong only if Davao uses/releases it for the target |
| Official final inspection, repair/retrofit acceptance, certificate of completion, or compliance acceptance with use/re-occupancy language | Strong if exact scope/date is clear |
| Signed/sealed structural stability or soundness certificate after earthquake/repairs | Answerable with caveat if no OCBO/LGU clearance accompanies it |

Not enough alone:

- repair permit
- approved repair methodology
- comprehensive structural assessment report submitted to OCBO
- Notice to Comply
- Compliance Report without authority acceptance
- PICE-assisted inspection mention without OCBO/LGU adoption
- owner/operator reopening statement
- business hours, bookings, mall reopening, tenant activity

## Overclaim Boundaries

- A Davao checklist proves required documents, not that a specific building
  passed review.
- A blank permit form is not evidence that a building was reviewed.
- A Building Permit or Certificate of Occupancy must be target-specific and tied
  to the relevant building/subscope.
- A red/yellow tag is date/scope-specific and must not become a permanent safety
  claim.
- A green tag, inspected placard, or clearance is date/scope-specific and must
  not become an earthquake-proof claim.
- A repair permit, repair methodology, or comprehensive assessment report is not
  clearance unless OCBO/LGU accepts it and allows use or re-occupancy.
- PICE involvement is technical support unless OCBO/LGU/DPWH adopts or publishes
  the result.
- Missing public records mean manual verification is needed, not that the
  building is unsafe, uncleared, unpermitted, or noncompliant.
